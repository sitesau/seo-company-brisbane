What to have ready before you request an audit

The audit is only as useful as what you bring to it. Before submitting the form, it is worth having your current site URL, your main Brisbane competitors by name, the suburb or service area you care most about, and the two or three keywords you already believe matter to your business. That gives the consultant a starting point instead of a blank brief.

  • Know which pages currently bring in enquiries, if any
  • List the suburbs or service areas you actually want to rank in
  • Have access to your site's analytics or search console, even basic, ready to share
  • Write down the one competitor whose rankings frustrate you most

None of that is complicated, but it turns a generic audit request into a targeted one.

Reading the audit before you commit

Once the audit lands, the useful test is whether it reads as a prioritised action list or a sales document. The site states the audit should give a realistic timeline before any work starts and no call centre handoff, which is the standard to hold it to. If the audit only lists problems without ranking them by impact, that is worth questioning before agreeing to a retainer.

Common questions

How much does SEO cost in Brisbane? The live page says retainers typically run from $1,200 to $4,000+GST per month, depending on competition, scope and the number of target keywords. One off audits and short campaigns start from around $800.

How long does SEO take to work? The live FAQ says most Brisbane businesses see measurable ranking movement in 3 to 6 months for mid competition keywords, while tougher terms in law, finance or national retail can take 9 to 12 months.

Does the service cover local SEO for specific suburbs? Yes. The page says local SEO covers Google Business Profile work, local directory listings, suburb level landing pages and review strategy so a business can show in map and local pack results across its service area.

What exactly does the audit look at? The page lists crawlability, indexation, Core Web Vitals, on-page tune-ups, internal link structure, backlink profile and the top three competitor gaps for the main keyword, delivered as a written action list.

Will any provider guarantee first page rankings? No. The site says no reputable SEO specialist guarantees rankings, since Google's algorithm is the only authority on that. It points instead to a transparent method and realistic timelines set before work begins.
